Background
  • In clinical trials, subjects would be the given the investigational drug or a matching control and we call these as 'study treatments' and relevant information for these medications is captured as part of EC/EX domains
  • Any other medications taken prior or concurrent with the study or during the follow-up phase of study are reported in CM domain
  • Like the concomitant medications, patients may undergo some procedures which may have diagnostic, preventive, therapeutic, or palliative effects
  • Some example procedures:
    • CT Scan of body
    • Biopsy
    • Wisdom teeth extraction
    • Eye examination
    • Endoscopy
    • Radiotherapy
    • Echocardiogram
  • SDTM standard has the domain named "Procedures (PR)" to capture the occurrence of these procedures
  • Any results coming out of these procedures should be captured in their own individual findings or findings about domains
    • Example, the occurrence of echocardiogram procedure is captured in PR and the resulting findings like left ventricular ejection fraction and right ventricular ejection fraction are to be reported in CV domain

What type of information is typically collected or presented?
  • Procedure name
  • Is the procedure prespecified?
  • If prespecified
    • Performed
    • Did not perform
      • Reason not performed
  • Date or start date of the procedure
  • Status of the procedure - ongoing or ended?
    • If ended, end date/time of the medication
  • Dose and dosing unit where applicable (e.g. radiotherapy intensity)
  • Frequency of administration (once daily/twice daily etc.)
  • Route of administration 
  • Location and laterality of the procedure (Hand/Leg - right/left)
  • Indication for which the procedure is performed
